Daily Expression:

Whether you just had a long day at work or school, setting time aside for yourself to express your creativity (in any form) is helpful. This allows for you to reconnect with yourself and get the juices flowing. It can also help reduce stress after a long day. The art doesn’t have to be significant, as it can just be a couple doodles.

Out with the old, in with the new

Treat yourself to new supplies. This may inspire you to work on your art more often.





Make Mistakes

Art isn’t perfect and neither are you. It’s important to remember this when making mistakes because it is not necessarily a bad thing. The best way to learn and become better is to make mistakes.





Be Intuitive

Not every part of your art has to be perfect. Over-analyzing your art can create a block in creativity and a negative response.

Caption: Change image





Find what motivates you

Are you motivated by plans or a treat? Figure out what motivates you and add it into your work.





Try Something New

Try a new medium, or start smaller by trying a new aspect within your already used medium. This may help you get what you’ve been looking for.





Participate

T find contests or even just a show. Whether you’re the artist or the audience, this can spark some inspiration.
